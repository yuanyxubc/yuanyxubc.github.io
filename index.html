About

Researcher in interactive media, game-based learning, and cross-cultural HCI

I study how interactive media and games can bridge cultural knowledge systems, foster ecological awareness, and support participatory learning — working at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction, game design, and sustainability.

Born and raised in Shanghai, I am now a doctoral researcher at UBC, co-creating educational games with Syilx (Okanagan) communities to mobilise diverse water knowledge and cultivate environmental resilience among youth. This work weaves together Indigenous protocols of dialogue, participatory design methods, and game-based learning, informed by my own Wu-Yue heritage from the Yangtze Delta and its eroding water memories.

Before the PhD, I trained in UX and cross-cultural interaction design, worked as a Product Manager at SAP Labs and Siemens, and completed professional training in the Canadian game industry. My research-creation portfolio includes 3D animation, AR game design, and immersive installations exhibited internationally. Earlier design work on inclusive technology was featured as a front-page headline in Shanghai Daily (2019).

Doctoral Research

Co-Designing Educational Games for Okanagan Water Knowledge
Ph.D. in Interdisciplinary Studies · UBC · 2025–2029

My doctoral project investigates how educational games can serve as platforms for mobilising diverse water knowledge — bridging Western scientific understanding with Indigenous (Syilx/Okanagan) ecological wisdom. Through participatory co-design with community members, educators, and youth, I am developing game-based learning tools that cultivate environmental resilience and cross-cultural dialogue around water governance in the Okanagan Valley.

This research is situated within the broader Beaver Worlds Project at UBC, which examines Okanagan water governance and Indigenous knowledge mobilisation. I am co-supervised by Prof. Aleksandra Dulic and Prof. Patricia Lasserre.

Research Themes

Game-Based Learning & Environmental Resilience
How can serious games foster ecological empathy, systems thinking, and pro-environmental behaviour? My systematic review (Sustainability, 2026) maps the state of the field and identifies design principles for effective eco-games.
Participatory Design with Indigenous Communities
Co-creating interactive media with Syilx (Okanagan) communities through culturally grounded protocols of dialogue and reciprocity — centering Indigenous sovereignty over knowledge representation in digital spaces.
Cross-Cultural HCI
Investigating how cultural context shapes human-computer interaction: from LLM conversational agents across cultures to cross-cultural game character design, informed by my own bilingual/bicultural background (China–Canada).

Methodological Expertise

Participatory Design Co-Design Game-Based Research Systematic Review Semantic Network Analysis Eye-Tracking Studies Kansei Engineering User Experience Research Research-Creation

Future Directions

My long-term research agenda centres on building a framework for culturally responsive game design that can be adapted across diverse ecological and cultural contexts — from the Okanagan Valley's water systems to the Yangtze Delta's eroding water memories. I aim to develop design methodologies, tools, and curricula that equip educators, designers, and communities to co-create games that honour local knowledge while fostering environmental stewardship.

I am particularly interested in how AI-enhanced design tools can support cross-cultural collaboration in game development, and how interactive media can bridge the gap between scientific data and lived ecological experience.
